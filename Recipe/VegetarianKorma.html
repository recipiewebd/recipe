Vegetarian Korma

Vegetarian Korma is a rich, creamy, and mildly spiced Indian curry made with a variety of vegetables, coconut milk, and a fragrant spice blend. This version offers a balanced sweetness from coconut and richness from yogurt or cream.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil or ghee
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 3 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt (or coconut milk for vegan option)
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, potatoes, cauliflower, peas)
  • 1/2 cup cashews, soaked and blended into a paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Juice of 1 lemon

Directions

Step 1: Sauté the Aromatics

Heat oil or ghee in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until golden brown, about 6-7 minutes. Add minced garlic and grated ginger, and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

Step 2: Add Spices and Tomato

Stir in cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, and chili powder. Sauté the spices for 2 minutes to release their aroma. Add the pureed tomatoes and cook for 5 minutes until the oil separates.

Step 3: Cook the Vegetables

Add the mixed vegetables and stir to coat them with the spice mixture. Cover and cook for 10 minutes until the vegetables begin to soften.

Step 4: Add Cashew Paste and Yogurt

Stir in the cashew paste and yogurt (or coconut milk for a vegan option). Add salt and simmer for another 10 minutes until the vegetables are cooked through and the korma is creamy.

Step 5: Garnish and Serve

Once the korma is ready, stir in lemon juice and garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve hot with basmati rice or naan.

Chef's Notes

  • For a richer korma, use cream instead of yogurt or coconut milk.
  • Soaked cashews add creaminess, but you can also use almond or cashew butter for convenience.
  • This dish can be made vegan by using coconut milk and skipping the yogurt.
  • Adjust the spice level by increasing or decreasing the chili powder to taste.

Air Fryer Pakoras

Air Fryer Pakoras are a healthier twist on the traditional Indian fritters, made with chickpea flour and a variety of vegetables. They are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, perfect for snacking or as an appetizer.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • 1 medium potato, thinly sliced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup spinach, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water, as needed
  • Cooking spray

Directions

Step 1

In a bowl, combine chickpea flour, cumin seeds, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Gradually add water to make a smooth batter.

Step 2

Add sliced potatoes, onions, spinach, and green chilies to the batter, mixing well to coat all the vegetables.

Step 3

Preheat the air fryer to 350°F (175°C). Lightly spray the basket with cooking spray.

Step 4

Drop spoonfuls of the vegetable batter mixture into the air fryer basket, ensuring they are not overcrowded.

Step 5

Air fry for about 12-15 minutes, flipping halfway through, until pakoras are golden and crispy.

Step 6

Serve hot with chutney or your favorite dipping sauce.

Chef's Notes

  • Feel free to add other vegetables like cauliflower or bell peppers for more variety.
  • For extra flavor, you can add spices like garam masala or ajwain (carom seeds) to the batter.

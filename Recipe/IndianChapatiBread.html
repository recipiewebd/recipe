Indian Chapati Bread

Chapati is a simple, traditional Indian flatbread made with whole wheat flour, water, and salt. This easy recipe requires no yeast or leavening agent, making it a quick and versatile bread to pair with curries, lentils, or simply enjoy with butter.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup warm water (or as needed)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (optional)

Directions

Step 1

In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt. Gradually add warm water, a little at a time, mixing with your hands until a soft dough forms. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes until smooth and elastic. If using, knead in the vegetable oil. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 20-30 minutes.

Step 2

Divide the dough into small balls (about 8-10). On a lightly floured surface, roll each ball into a thin, round disc about 6-7 inches in diameter. Repeat with the remaining dough.

Step 3

Heat a skillet or griddle over medium-high heat. Place a rolled-out chapati on the skillet and cook for about 30 seconds, until small bubbles appear. Flip and cook the other side for another 30 seconds. Press down gently with a spatula to help puff up the chapati. Continue flipping until both sides have light brown spots, and the chapati is fully cooked.

Step 4

Remove from the skillet and keep warm in a towel-lined basket. Repeat with the remaining dough. Serve warm with curry, dal, or as desired.

  • For extra softness, you can brush the chapatis with ghee or butter immediately after cooking.

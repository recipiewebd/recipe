Kheer (Rice Pudding)

This traditional Indian Kheer, a rice pudding dish, is made with rice simmered in milk and sweetened with sugar. Flavored with cardamom and garnished with chopped nuts, it is a creamy dessert that is served during festivals or special occasions. The richness of milk and the subtle sweetness make it a perfect dessert for all ages.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup basmati rice
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 2 tablespoons chopped almonds
  • 2 tablespoons chopped cashews
  • 2 tablespoons raisins
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)
  • Pinch of saffron strands (optional)

Directions

Step 1

Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain the rice and set aside.

Step 2

In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat ghee over medium heat. Add the chopped almonds, cashews, and raisins. Sauté until golden brown and set aside.

Step 3

Add the drained rice to the pot and sauté for a few minutes. Pour in the milk, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer, stirring occasionally, until the rice is cooked and the mixture thickens (about 25-30 minutes).

Step 4

Stir in the sugar and cardamom powder. Continue cooking for another 5-10 minutes, until the sugar is dissolved and the kheer is creamy. Add saffron strands if using.

Step 5

Turn off the heat, and garnish with the sautéed nuts and raisins. Serve warm or chilled, depending on preference.

Chef's Notes

  • You can use other varieties of long grain rice, but adjust cooking times accordingly.
  • Kheer thickens as it cools, so adjust the consistency with extra milk if needed before serving.
  • This recipe yields 6 servings and is best enjoyed fresh but can be refrigerated for up to 2 days.
  • For a richer flavor, use condensed milk instead of regular milk.

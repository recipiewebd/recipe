Crispy Vegetable Pakodas

Crispy Vegetable Pakodas are deep-fried fritters made with a mixture of vegetables and spiced chickpea flour batter, creating a crunchy snack. This version is packed with shredded veggies and spices for extra flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • 1/4 cup rice flour (for extra crispiness)
  • 1 medium potato, shredded
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped spinach or cabbage
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/4 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • Oil for frying
  • Chopped fresh cilantro for garnish

Directions

Step 1: Prepare the Batter

In a large mixing bowl, combine chickpea flour, rice flour, cumin seeds, coriander powder, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Gradually add water to make a thick batter.

Step 2: Add Vegetables

Stir in the shredded potato, onions, carrots, spinach, and green chilies (if using). Mix well to coat the vegetables with the batter. Let it rest for 5 minutes.

Step 3: Fry the Pakodas

Heat oil in a deep pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, drop spoonfuls of the vegetable batter into the oil, taking care not to overcrowd the pan. Fry until golden brown and crispy, about 4-5 minutes per batch. Drain on paper towels.

Step 4: Serve

Garnish the pakodas with fresh cilantro and serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind sauce.

Chef's Notes

  • For extra crunch, add crushed peanuts or sesame seeds to the batter.
  • You can air-fry pakodas at 180°C (350°F) for a healthier option, brushing them with a bit of oil.
  • Adjust the spice level by modifying the amount of green chilies and red chili powder.

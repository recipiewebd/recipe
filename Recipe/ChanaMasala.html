Chana Masala

Chana Masala is a flavorful, tangy chickpea curry with an aromatic spice blend. This version includes roasted tomatoes and a hint of amchur powder (dried mango) for an authentic, slightly sour kick.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas (or 1 can, drained and rinsed)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, roasted and pureed (new twist for deeper flavor)
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon amchur powder (dried mango, for a tangy twist)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro (plus more for garnish)
  • Juice of 1 lemon

Directions

Step 1: Prepare the Base

Heat oil or ghee in a large skillet over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds until fragrant. Add the chopped onion and sauté until golden brown, about 5 minutes.

Step 2: Add Garlic, Ginger, and Spices

Stir in the minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies, and sauté for another minute. Then add garam masala, cumin, coriander, turmeric, and red chili powder. Cook for 2 minutes until the spices release their aroma.

Step 3: Add Tomatoes

Add the roasted and pureed tomatoes to the skillet. Stir well and cook for 5-7 minutes until the oil separates from the masala base. This step deepens the flavor of the curry.

Step 4: Add Chickpeas and Simmer

Stir in the cooked chickpeas and 1 cup of water (or more for desired consistency). Add salt and amchur powder. Simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the chickpeas to absorb the flavors.

Step 5: Finish and Garnish

Once the curry thickens slightly, stir in lemon juice and cilantro. Adjust seasoning if necessary. Garnish with more fresh cilantro before serving.

Chef's Notes

  • Roasting the tomatoes before pureeing them adds a smoky, caramelized flavor to the dish.
  • Amchur powder provides an authentic tang to Chana Masala, but you can substitute with lemon juice if unavailable.
  • For a thicker consistency, mash some chickpeas before simmering.
  • Serve with rice or naan for a complete meal.

Chai Tea Latte

Chai Tea Latte is a spiced, warming drink made with brewed black tea and a fragrant blend of spices, mixed with steamed milk and topped with froth for a rich and comforting beverage.

Ingredients

  • 2 black tea bags (or loose leaf tea)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup milk (dairy or non-dairy like almond or oat milk)
  • 2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup (optional, for sweetness)
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4-5 cardamom pods, lightly crushed
  • 4-5 whole cloves
  • 1 small piece of fresh ginger (about 1 inch), sliced
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Ground cinnamon (for garnish)

Directions

Step 1: Brew the Tea

In a medium saucepan, bring 2 cups of water to a boil. Add the black tea bags, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, cloves, ginger, and nutmeg. Reduce the heat and simmer for 5 minutes, allowing the tea and spices to infuse.

Step 2: Strain the Tea

Strain the tea into a cup, discarding the spices and tea bags. If you prefer a sweeter latte, stir in honey or maple syrup to taste.

Step 3: Prepare the Milk

In the same saucepan, heat the milk over medium heat until warm but not boiling. Stir in the vanilla extract. Optionally, froth the milk using a milk frother or whisk to create foam.

Step 4: Combine Tea and Milk

Pour the warm milk over the brewed tea. Top with frothy milk, if desired. Sprinkle with ground cinnamon for garnish.

Chef's Notes

  • Adjust the spice levels based on your taste. For a spicier chai, add more ginger or cardamom.
  • For a dairy-free version, use almond, oat, or coconut milk.
  • This recipe can be customized with different sweeteners like honey, agave, or stevia.

Chicken Tikka Masala

A beloved classic with an exciting twist! Enjoy tender chicken marinated in spices, grilled, and simmered in a creamy tomato sauce with an added touch of roasted bell peppers and a hint of smoked paprika for a depth of flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) boneless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons smoked paprika (new addition for smoky flavor)
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 2 teaspoons minced fresh ginger
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped (new addition for extra flavor and texture)
  • 2 cups tomato puree
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream or coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro (plus more for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon butter or ghee
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Directions

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

In a bowl, combine the yogurt, lemon juice, smoked paprika, garam masala, cumin, coriander, ginger, and half the garlic. Mix well and add the chicken pieces, ensuring they're fully coated. Cover and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour (or overnight for deeper flavor).

Step 2: Grill or Sear the Chicken

Preheat your grill or heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade and grill or sear for 3-4 minutes per side until lightly charred and cooked through. Set aside.

Step 3: Prepare the Masala Sauce

In the same skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and red bell pepper, and cook until soft and golden, about 5 minutes. Stir in the remaining garlic and cook for another minute.

Step 4: Add Spices and Tomatoes

Stir in the tomato puree and simmer for 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld. Add salt and pepper to taste. For a deeper flavor, add 1 more teaspoon of smoked paprika (optional).

Step 5: Finish with Cream and Chicken

Stir in the heavy cream (or coconut milk) and bring to a gentle simmer. Add the grilled chicken and let it simmer in the sauce for an additional 5 minutes. Adjust seasoning if needed.

Step 6: Serve and Garnish

Remove from heat and garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve hot with basmati rice or naan bread.

Chef's Notes

  • Adding smoked paprika and roasted bell peppers gives the dish an added depth of flavor that balances with the creaminess.
  • You can replace the heavy cream with coconut milk for a dairy-free version.
  • For a spicier version, add green chilies or increase the amount of garam masala.

Chef John's Chicken Tikka Masala

This Chicken Tikka Masala recipe features tender, spiced chicken pieces in a creamy, aromatic tomato sauce. The dish is rich with flavor and a perfect balance of spices, making it a classic favorite in Indian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds skinless, boneless chicken thighs
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 (28-ounce) can tomato puree
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Chopped fresh cilantro for garnish

Directions

Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

In a large bowl, combine yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, ginger, cumin, turmeric, garam masala, coriander, paprika, and salt. Add the chicken thighs, ensuring they are well coated with the marinade. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably up to 3 hours for maximum flavor.

Step 2: Cook the Chicken

Preheat your grill or oven to 400°F (200°C). Remove the chicken from the marinade and shake off excess. Grill or bake the chicken for about 10–12 minutes, turning halfway through, until charred and cooked through (internal temperature should reach 165°F). Set aside.

Step 3: Prepare the Sauce

In a large skillet, heat oil over medium heat. Add diced onions and sauté for 8–10 minutes until golden brown. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes. Add the tomato puree, paprika, and garam masala. Simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens.

Step 4: Finish the Sauce

Lower the heat and add the heavy cream, stirring well to combine. Simmer for another 5 minutes until the sauce is smooth and creamy.

Step 5: Combine Chicken and Sauce

Add the cooked chicken to the sauce, stirring gently to coat the chicken. Simmer for an additional 5–10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

Step 6: Serve

Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice or naan.

Chef's Notes

  • Marinating the chicken for at least 3 hours enhances the flavor. If short on time, 1 hour works, but more time is better.
  • Grill or bake the chicken until slightly charred for an authentic taste. You can also broil or pan-sear if grilling isn’t an option.
  • Heavy cream gives the sauce a rich texture, but you can substitute with coconut milk for a dairy-free option.
